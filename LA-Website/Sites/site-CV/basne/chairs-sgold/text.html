CHAIRS

Samuel Gold | Báseň

The chairs running through the street
Through the drains of what we called humanity
To the grasps of wailing men
Waiting for pleasure yet so blind

A crowd gathers around dusk
Watching a man in the middle fail
Crying and weeping everyone laughing
Dropping down his books one by one
Considering his idol's luxury
Thinking a replica for himself

A man lost in the winds of society
Blaming his reckless drinking and smoking on them
To the blistering reality of rejection
Rejection which occurs unconditionally
His only loyal bystander
Disappointment becomes a norm

And yet he strides forward
Strides for failure and disappointment
Studying endlessly in a cycle
Expected for medicine or law
Free yet blinded by beliefs and morals
Suggesting his opinions to be clichÃ©

Dreaming to become a chair
The chair that sits on people
Running and killing yet fulfilling
From the castle to a random house
From culture and history
A peak to a life of mystery
Full of genius minds intoxicated by joy
Seizing the day and becoming superman

Contemplating rap and drums
Forgetting the uninvited
The chair moves on slowly yet steadily
Running to the deepest corners of love
Seeing failure as a success
A wooden heart nevertheless

Lying to his closest justified by career
Imagine a world dominated by fear
Fear of the unknown and strange
Brings one's mind into rage
God created man and man created chair
Hidden in it eternal lair

Forgotten and found in times of trust
Betrayed by its own lust
The chair saw mountains and talent
Dark snow with frozen white rain
The white beaches of plastic
The sand of doom

Yet the young remain stupid and blind
A lie repeated constantly becomes reality
Young waging war with likes
Ignoring there won't be one to wage
Blind and naive as chairs
Falling down without a backbone
Manipulated and excluded
Problematic yet never recruited

Lost potential and ruin
Awaits the chairs soon
Useless as no Wi-Fi and a talking baboon
Yet hope is the equivalent to fair
Sunk in our morals like a chair
